# Harry's Discovery Tips

## Persuading people to take a call

- **Make your first message personal.** Ask this tool to personalise it, but honestly it will likely need some degree of manual editing to get right.
- **Cold outreach insight is the most valuable.** Insight from cold outreach to unknowns is better than 2nd degree connections, which is better than 1st degree connections. Expect response rates to be low from cold outreach, so bolster with first and second degree connections — but the uptake from cold outreach is signal in itself. Try and track which positioning resonates most with cold outreach.
- **Be direct and concise.** LinkedIn's 250 char limit on messages appended to connection requests is the perfect length to establish credibility, get the message across and deliver an ask.
- **The real secret: offer advisory positions.** This is a great way to get senior people interested in talking to you. Add something like "we are also looking for high-value advisors for the business" to the end of your intro message. This is not duplicitous — if there's a good fit and they give good advice, you might want to bring them on as an advisor. If you do suggest it in the intro, make sure you mention it at the end of the call too.

## On the call

- **Dig into problem severity.**
  - How often do they have this problem? Once a day/week/month? Focus on ICPs and problems that are happening at least weekly, ideally daily.
  - Ask: **"Is this on your to-do list to solve right now?"** This is a killer question. You move from selling something to helping someone tick something off their to-do list — which is MUCH easier and therefore great signal you're working on something valuable.
- **Always ask who you should speak to next** — and ideally for an intro.
- **Always ask to keep them in the loop.** These people will be a good source of first paying customers.

## Generally

- **You must try to be as objective as possible.** It's so easy to fall into confirmation bias traps.
